Privacy Policy

Effective Date: May 16, 2025

Site: https://palaseus.github.io/public-notices/index.html

I. Preface

This Privacy Policy is a declaration of our absolute commitment to privacy, user dignity, and informational non-interference. It applies solely to the aforementioned site (“Site”), hosted via GitHub Pages under the stewardship of its creator. The creator and maintainer of this Site do not operate under the pretense of surveillance capitalism, targeted advertising, or any form of user manipulation or profiling. This document serves as an express repudiation of the normalization of digital tracking and commodified attention.

II. Data Collection: None

We collect absolutely no personally identifiable information (PII) from visitors. This Site does not request, log, store, transmit, or otherwise process:

  • Names
  • Email addresses
  • IP addresses
  • MAC addresses
  • Device IDs
  • Geographic locations
  • Biometric or behavioral data
  • Search history
  • Form submissions (there are none)
  • Session data
  • Referrer or browser metadata

Furthermore, there are:

  • No sign-ups
  • No user accounts
  • No analytics
  • No tracking scripts
  • No hidden beacons
  • No third-party embeds
  • No social media pixels or plugins
  • No cookies of any kind — neither first-party nor third-party

III. JavaScript-Free Environment

This Site does not use or rely upon JavaScript. Every page is rendered in static HTML/CSS only. There are no active or passive client-side scripts executing during or after page load. This guarantees that:

  • Nothing runs in your browser
  • No telemetry or interaction data is captured
  • You are not profiled, fingerprinted, or exposed

Your web browser interacts solely with the static files served directly by GitHub’s content delivery network (CDN).

IV. Hosting Infrastructure

This Site is hosted on GitHub Pages, a free and static hosting service provided by GitHub, Inc., a subsidiary of Microsoft Corporation. While GitHub may maintain standard access logs for security and operational purposes (such as IP addresses for traffic routing), such logs are not accessible to or used by this Site's creator. We do not receive, store, or analyze this data. We neither control nor exploit GitHub's back-end infrastructure.

You are encouraged to review GitHub’s privacy policy if you wish to understand how GitHub, as a platform provider, handles incidental traffic data.

V. External Links and Third-Party Sites

This Site may contain hyperlinks to other websites for reference or citation. Clicking such links may direct you away from our domain. We do not endorse or assume responsibility for the privacy policies or content of any third-party websites. Once you leave our domain, you are subject to the terms and practices of the destination site, over which we exert no control.

We recommend reviewing the privacy policies of any external websites you visit, particularly if those sites require interaction, cookies, or data submission.

VI. No Consent Required, No Consent Taken

Unlike most websites, we do not ask for cookie consent because no cookies are in use. We do not ask for consent to collect data because no data is collected. Consent is not sought because intrusion is absent. This is a pure, non-interactive environment where you are a sovereign visitor — not a user to be mined.

VII. Children’s Privacy

We do not knowingly collect data from children or adults. This Site is informational in nature and designed for general public use. It contains no forms, no subscriptions, and no account features. There is nothing to opt into, nothing to sign, nothing to disclose.

VIII. Changes to This Policy

Since no data collection or tracking occurs, changes to this Privacy Policy are rare. However, if we ever amend the Site to include interactive components or data processing features (which is not currently intended), this Privacy Policy will be updated accordingly and posted prominently on the home page.

Changes, if any, will never be retroactive. Users will always retain the right to access the site without data collection or profiling.

IX. Contact

If you have questions, concerns, or feedback about this Privacy Policy, or wish to understand its intent or implications, you may contact the site maintainer directly via any channel publicly listed (if any). However, note that this site does not solicit or expect correspondence.

X. Final Declaration

This Privacy Policy is intentionally radical in its simplicity. It represents a philosophical and ethical stance against the mass commodification of human attention and the exploitation of personal data. You are free to read, study, and use the contents of this Site without fear of being watched, logged, or judged.

We view privacy not as a policy requirement, but as a moral right.

This Site collects nothing. This Site stores nothing. This Site knows nothing about you. And that is exactly the point.
